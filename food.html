Old Nigerian Delicacies

A celebration of traditional Nigerian dishes passed down through generations

Classic Nigerian Dishes

Discover the flavors and stories behind Nigeria's most cherished traditional meals

Pounded Yam & Egusi Soup

Pounded yam, a staple among the Yoruba and Igbo, is made by pounding boiled yam until smooth. Egusi soup, crafted from melon seeds, is enjoyed across Nigeria but has deep roots in the Yoruba and Igbo regions, symbolizing celebration and hospitality.

Jollof Rice

Jollof rice traces its origins to the Senegambian region, but Nigeria’s version became iconic through local adaptation. It is now a centerpiece at Nigerian gatherings, reflecting the country’s love for communal feasting and culinary creativity.

Ofada Rice & Ayamase

Ofada rice is indigenous to southwestern Nigeria, especially among the Yoruba. The spicy Ayamase stew, made with green peppers and assorted meats, is a hallmark of Yoruba festive cuisine.

Nkwobi

Nkwobi, a spicy cow foot dish, originates from the Igbo people of southeastern Nigeria. Traditionally served in local bars, it is a symbol of celebration and social gatherings.

Moin-Moin

Moin-Moin, a steamed bean pudding, is popular among the Yoruba but enjoyed nationwide. Its roots lie in the creative use of local beans, peppers, and spices, often wrapped in banana leaves for added flavor.

Ukodo (Yam Pepper Soup)

Ukodo, or yam pepper soup, is a specialty of the Urhobo people in southern Nigeria. Traditionally served at important occasions, it combines yam and spicy broth for warmth and comfort.

Top 3 Nigerian Delicacies & Their Ingredients

Explore the essential ingredients behind Nigeria's most beloved traditional dishes.

Jollof Rice

  • Long grain rice
  • Tomatoes & tomato paste
  • Red bell peppers
  • Onions
  • Scotch bonnet peppers
  • Vegetable oil
  • Chicken or beef stock
  • Seasonings (thyme, curry, bay leaf, salt, bouillon)

Pounded Yam & Egusi Soup

  • Yam tubers (for pounded yam)
  • Egusi (melon seeds)
  • Palm oil
  • Spinach or bitterleaf
  • Assorted meats (beef, goat, tripe)
  • Stockfish or dried fish
  • Ground crayfish
  • Onions, pepper, seasoning cubes, salt

Moin-Moin

  • Black-eyed beans
  • Red bell peppers
  • Onions
  • Scotch bonnet peppers
  • Palm oil or vegetable oil
  • Seasoning cubes, salt
  • Optional: boiled eggs, sardines, corned beef
  • Banana leaves or ramekins for steaming
