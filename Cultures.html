Culture of the Week: Yoruba

Spotlighting the vibrant Yoruba culture, its traditions, language, and influence across Nigeria and the diaspora.

Key Features

  • Language: Yoruba
  • Popular Festivals: Osun-Osogbo, Eyo, Olojo
  • Traditional Attire: Aso-Oke, Agbada, Gele
  • Famous Cuisine: Amala, Ewedu, Ofada rice
  • Arts: Beadwork, Batik, Bronze heads

Yoruba culture is renowned for its storytelling, music, and elaborate ceremonies. Its influence can be seen in art, religion, and language across West Africa and beyond.

Popular Nigerian Proverbs

Discover the wisdom of Nigeria’s cultures through their most famous proverbs and meanings.

Yoruba

"Bí a kó bá mọ ìtàn ìbí a tí ńbọ, a kò lè mọ ìbí tí a ńlọ."

("If you don’t know where you are coming from, you won’t know where you are going.")

Meaning: Value your roots and history to guide your future.

Igbo

"A kụkọtara ọnụ, e mee ka ọ dị mma."

("When people come together, things get better.")

Meaning: Unity and collaboration bring progress.

Hausa

"Ruwa baya tsami da magani."

("Water does not get sour with medicine.")

Meaning: Good advice or help never spoils a situation.

Edo

"Oba nor dey go transfer."

("The king does not get transferred.")

Meaning: True leadership is rooted and stable.

Tiv

"Aondo u sha u dedoo."

("God’s time is the best.")

Meaning: Patience brings good results; trust in divine timing.

Ibibio

"Ekpe ke ekpe, ekpe ke idem."

("A monkey does not forget its own kind.")

Meaning: People should remember and support their community.
