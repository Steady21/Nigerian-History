Nigeria's Historical Timeline

Pre-Colonial Era (Before 1800)

Nigeria was home to several advanced civilizations including the Nok culture (1500 BCE), the Benin Kingdom, and the Yoruba city-states.

Ancient History

Colonial Period (1800-1960)

British influence expanded through the Royal Niger Company. In 1914, the Northern and Southern protectorates were amalgamated to form modern Nigeria.

Colonialism

Independence Era (1960-Present)

Nigeria gained independence on October 1, 1960. The country has since experienced periods of civilian and military rule, civil war, and transition to democracy.

Modern Era

Cultural Heritage

Yoruba Traditions

The Yoruba people are known for their rich artistic traditions, including sculpture, beadwork, textiles, and the Ifa divination system.

Influential Figures

Dr. Nnamdi Azikiwe

(1904-1996) First President of Nigeria

A leading figure in Nigeria's independence movement and prominent nationalist who became the country's first indigenous Governor-General.

Sir Ahmadu Bello

(1910-1966) Premier of Northern Nigeria

The Sardauna of Sokoto was a key political and religious leader who championed northern interests during Nigeria's transition to independence.

Funmilayo Ransome-Kuti

(1900-1978) Women's Rights Activist

A pioneering feminist who led women's resistance against colonial taxation policies and founded the Abeokuta Women's Union.
