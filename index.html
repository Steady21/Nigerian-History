Did You Know?

Discover fascinating facts about Nigerians who started trends, sayings, or cultural movements!

Chinua Achebe

Did you know Chinua Achebe popularized the phrase "Things Fall Apart" with his 1958 novel, now a global saying for sudden change?

#Literature

Fela Kuti

Fela Kuti started the Afrobeat music genre and the phrase "Music is the weapon," inspiring global activism through sound.

#Music

Maryam Babangida

Maryam Babangida launched the "Better Life for Rural Women" program, sparking the trend of women empowerment initiatives in Nigeria.

#Empowerment

Herbert Macaulay

Herbert Macaulay is credited with starting the phrase "Freedom for Nigeria," which became a rallying cry for independence.

#History

Funmilayo Ransome-Kuti

Funmilayo Ransome-Kuti led the first major women's protest in Nigeria, inspiring the saying "Women's voice matters" still used today.

#Activism

Chief Zebrudaya

Chief Zebrudaya, from the sitcom "New Masquerade," made the phrase "No wahala!" a popular Nigerian way to say "No problem."

#PopCulture
